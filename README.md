##Customer Purchase Behavior Analysis for Targeted Marketing Strategy

Dataset: Kaggle – 1500 rows × 9 columns
Target Variable: Purchase Status (Indicates whether a customer is likely to buy)

##Dataset Overview
The dataset includes demographic and behavioral details:
Age, Gender, Annual Income, Number of Purchases
Product Category, Time Spent on Website, Loyalty Program, Discounts Availed
These features help identify customer profiles, buying patterns, and engagement factors.

##Project Objective
Analyze customer purchase data to:
Understand customer behavior and preferences
Segment customers based on likelihood to purchase
Provide actionable insights for targeted marketing strategies

##Approach
Data Cleaning & Analysis – Python (Pandas, NumPy)
Exploratory Data Analysis – Identify patterns in demographics, income, loyalty, and discounts
Predictive Modeling – Classification-based machine learning:
AdaBoost Classifier achieved the best accuracy
Visualization – Tableau dashboards for insights on customer segments
Interactive Application – Streamlit web app for exploring predictions and insights

##Key Insights
Customers aged 40–50 and 50+ are the largest purchasing groups → highly profitable
Higher income groups show higher purchase counts → strong spending capacity
Loyalty members contribute the major share of purchases → retention programs are effective
Discounts increase purchase likelihood → incentives drive conversions
Purchase Likelihood:
Likely buyers → 49.43%
Not likely buyers → 50.57% → Opportunity to improve marketing performance
Combining discounts + loyalty programs yields the best engagement results

##Business Impact
Helps businesses target the right customers
Improves marketing engagement and ROI
Enables data-driven decision-making via interactive dashboard

##Tools & Technologies
Python – Data cleaning & predictive modeling
Pandas & NumPy – Data handling and analysis
Tableau – Visualization of customer insights
Streamlit – Interactive prediction dashboard
AdaBoost Classifier – Machine learning model for customer segmentation
